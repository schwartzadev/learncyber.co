What is Identity Theft?

"Identity theft occurs when someone unlawfully obtains another's personal information and uses it to commit theft or fraud." - FBI

Under Title 18, U.S. Code, Section 1028, it is a federal crime to "knowingly transfer or use, without lawful authority, a means of identification of another person with the intent to commit, or to aid or abet, any unlawful activity that constitutes a violation of federal law, or that constitutes a felony under any applicable state or local law."
Or, simplified, it is a federal crime to share or use someone else's ID to do anything illegal. When someone breaks this law, it is considered identity theft. Identity theft can be theft of many types of things including names, birth dates, social security numbers, Medicare information, bank and credit card information, security phrases such as a user's mother's maiden name, and even fingerprint information.
Besides physical theft, criminals often use a method called phishing to obtain a user's information. This is where a criminal creates or clones a login page for a website (online banking, etc.) and displays their fake webpage on a user's screen instead of the actual one. This also allows them to steal the data the user puts in as their username and password through their fake website.

How Can You Protect Yourself?

As a user of any online service, you can protect yourself from phishing scams by always checking that you have the right URL before you sign into a page. Many browsers and companies have a padlock icon next to the website, and if this is there, it means you have a secure connection to the real company.
